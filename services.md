      <h2>Adversarial Readiness Review</h2>

      <p>
        The Adversarial Readiness Review is a strategic assessment of your security
        program through the lens of a real attacker.
      </p>

      <p>
        Instead of measuring compliance, policy completion, or control presence,
        this review evaluates how an adversary would assess your environment,
        identify weaknesses across key domains, and pursue the fastest or most
        reliable path to their objectives.
      </p>

      <p>
        Using a structured set of red-flag questions across areas such as:
      </p>

      <ul class="service-list">
        <li>Identity &amp; Access Management</li>
        <li>MFA / Identity Providers / Federation</li>
        <li>Application Security</li>
        <li>CI/CD &amp; Software Supply Chain</li>
        <li>Cloud &amp; Infrastructure</li>
        <li>External Attack Surface</li>
        <li>Detection &amp; Response</li>
        <li>Vulnerability Management</li>
        <li>Threat Intelligence</li>
        <li>Governance &amp; Security Metrics</li>
        <li>AI Security</li>
      </ul>

      <p>
        The ARR surfaces where controls may exist on paper but fail in practice,
        where ownership is unclear, where trust is misplaced, and where attackers
        are most likely to succeed.
      </p>
    </article>
